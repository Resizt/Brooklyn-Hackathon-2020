Services

Throuhgout the city there have been very generious services that assist the need and is always there giving back. Below are the programs that are listed to assist the need.

NYC DHS

This agency focuses on addressing homelessness and preventing it when possible by requiring their shelter population to actively look for employment with their support and connections. They also educate the homeless about saving money and how to find a permanent residence in the future.

NYC Shelters

The NYC shelter system is quite sophisticated and very populated. In 2019, 132 thousand different people slept in a shelter instead of a street, this is because there are no waitlists and usually no denials to entry at NYC shelters, unless ineligible.

Medicare/Medicaid for The Homeless

The Medicare system is based on a couple eligibility pathways that can grant you coverage, and in most cases homelessness fills out at least one eligibility pathway, low income. This makes the Medicare system a powerful tool for the homeless and less fortunate groups that live in statistically poorer areas of our country to gain access to healthcare coverage.

HUD

HUD provides a list of many resources, facilities and centers that are needed for many underprivileged groups such as homeless people, veterans, victims of natural disasters etc. Contacts for everything from low income housing to mental illness treatment is provided on this website in order to help the less fortunate.

Clothing Drop Offs

Living in a city where 1 in every 125 residents are homeless makes you question what you can be doing to help. The clothes drive is a simple thing that anyone can do, by bringing a couple of pieces of clothes you are not planning to wear can help the homeless during cold winters. Drop off locations are even marked for your convenience on their map.
